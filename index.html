Liquid Glass UI

The fluid future of interface design

Liquid glass balances the softness of organic motion with the precision of high-clarity glass panels. This prototype highlights how layered refractions, frosted light, and liquid gradients can build a tactile digital surface.

Refraction

Dynamic blur responds to pointer proximity, simulating a glass lens skimming across liquid.

Viscosity

Soft body ripples keep motion cohesive, so components appear to float in the same medium.

Clarity

High-contrast typography punctures through fogged layers for comfortable readability.

Color wells

Use saturated gradients that drift subtly to mimic liquid dispersion.

Depth script

Layer blur tokens to simulate parallax and refraction shifts.

  • mist-4 – 8px blur for touch targets
  • mist-8 – 16px blur for cards & tooltips
  • mist-16 – 32px blur for backdrop plates

Motion viscosities

Ease curves are designed to feel like liquid flexing behind glass.

Interface styleguide

Core guidance on typography, component spacing, and interaction detail ensures that every layer of the experience feels consistently fluid.

Typography rhythm

Pair bold headlines at 1.1 line height with body text set to 1.6 to keep the glass panels airy without losing focus.

H1 – Manrope 64 Body – Manrope 18 Caption – Manrope 14

Spacing fluidity

Structure layouts with an 8px base grid, expanding to 24px and 48px steps for macro spacing to mirror liquid tension.

  • 8px micro control targets
  • 24px component gutters
  • 48px section breathing room

Interaction polish

Layer light halos and motion blur on interactive states so buttons feel suspended above the liquid plane.
